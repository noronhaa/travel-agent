# 🌍 Lombok Travel Brief — May

## 📋 Overview
Lombok in May is a serene paradise perfect for those seeking adventure and relaxation. With its less crowded beaches compared to Bali, it's an ideal destination for surfers, nature enthusiasts, and digital nomads. The island offers stunning surf spots, breathtaking waterfalls, and cozy cafes perfect for working remotely. Lombok suits travelers looking for a mix of adventure and tranquility without the bustling tourist crowds.

## 🌤️ Weather & Packing
- **Temperature**: Average temperatures range from 25°C to 32°C (77°F to 89°F).
- **Conditions**: May marks the beginning of the dry season, with occasional showers but generally clear skies.
- **What to Pack**: Lightweight clothing, swimwear, a light rain jacket, sunscreen, insect repellent, and a hat. Don't forget your surf gear and a power bank for working in cafes.

## 🏨 Where to Stay
- **Kuta**: Known for its vibrant surf culture and stunning beaches. Great for surfers and digital nomads. Budget accommodations start at IDR 200,000 (£10) per night.
- **Senggigi**: Offers beautiful sunsets and a more laid-back vibe. Ideal for those looking to relax after surfing. Mid-range hotels start at IDR 500,000 (£25) per night.
- **Mangsit**: A quieter area with lush surroundings, perfect for nature lovers. Budget accommodations start at IDR 300,000 (£15) per night.

## 🎯 Top Experiences
1. **Surfing at Mawi Beach**: Known for its consistent waves, it's a must-visit for surfers. No entry fee.
2. **Tiu Kelep Waterfall**: A stunning waterfall located in the north of Lombok. Entry fee: IDR 10,000 (£0.50).
3. **Benang Kelambu Waterfall**: A picturesque waterfall surrounded by lush greenery. Entry fee: IDR 15,000 (£0.75).
4. **Terra - Plant Based Food Café**: A popular spot for digital nomads with fast Wi-Fi and a great menu.
5. **Kenza Café**: Offers a cozy atmosphere with outlets for laptops and a healthy menu, perfect for remote work.

## ✈️ Getting There from Nusa Lembongan
- **Ferry**: The most common option, costing between IDR 270,000 to IDR 750,000 (£14 to £38). Travel time is approximately 2.5 to 3 hours.
- **Flight**: Though less direct, flights are available from Bali to Lombok, costing around IDR 650,000 (£33). Total travel time, including transfers, is about 4 hours.
- **Booking Tip**: Book ferries in advance during the dry season to secure your spot.
